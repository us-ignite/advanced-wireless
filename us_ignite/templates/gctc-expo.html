Global City Teams Challenge Expo

Join US Ignite and NIST June 1st in Washington for the Global City Teams Challenge Expo to see the future of Smart City / Internet of Things technology.

US Ignite and NIST, with the help of our partners, will showcase new IoT technologies that are transforming decision making within a smart city / smart community environment here in the US and around the world.

Please join us on June 1, 2015 for an international showcase of innovative Smart City and Internet of Things applications featuring:

  • Project demonstrations from 40+ GCTC teams offering a chance to see and touch key components of unmanned aerial vehicle networks for firefighters, autonomous vehicles and other Internet of Things applications
  • Rapid-fire presentations delivered by city officials and team members researching and deploying technologies related to sectors including healthcare, energy and disaster recovery among others.
  • Opportunities to engage thought leaders from industry, government and the university research sectors as well as 30+ participating cities around the world
  • Keynotes and announcements regarding opportunities for municipalities and innovators to participate in smart city initiatives in 2015-2016 and beyond

Who Should Attend the GCTC Expo?

  • Mayors, CIOs, CTOs, Innovation Directors, Managers from local, state, federal governments working on smart city deployments
  • Corporations, educational institutions, non-profits with an interest in smart city projects.
  • Members of the media
  • All parties interested in participating in Smart City and IoT collaborations
  • Students, teachers and others curious about the Internet of Things and Smart City applications

Questions about GCTC Expo? Please email sokwoo.rhee@nist.gov or william.maguire@us-ignite.org.

  • CyDECS: Cyber Defense Exercise for Critical Infrastructure Security (Iowa) - This project develops new tools to protect critical infrastructures from cyber attacks.

  • Using Dynamic Virtualization and Intelligent Edge Devices in Disaster Response - The goal of this project is to integrate data from sensors and cameras to facilitate disaster response.
  • 5D Smart San Francisco 2030 District - This project creates a central hub of data necessary to enhance sustainability in the Area of Interest.
  • Bringing Internet of Things Know-How to High School Students - This project integrates lessons in the Internet of Things to high school classrooms.
  • Integrating an Aerial Base Station with a City’s Emergency Communication Grid - This initiative coordinates data from an aerial base station with the City of Denton’s emergency communication grid.
  • Ecosystem for Smart Medical Simulation Team Training - This project utilizes dynamic data collection to improve the effectiveness of medical and emergency response teams.
  • Energy Storage based Adaptive Demand Response in Smart Commercial Buildings - The goal of this endeavor is to demonstrate how better energy storage can reduce grid stress and improve energy efficiency.
  • Mobile-first Communication and Service On Demand Infrastructure for Hospitality - This project utilizes a dynamic data collection system to improve staff effectiveness in hotels.
  • ParaDrop: City-scale Analytics - This initiative will employ ParaDrop Access Points to provide useful information for city planning and businesses.
  • SENAS: A Smart City Educational Network for Autistic Students - This system brings together available resources to improve educational opportunities for autistic students.

  • Resource Network for Early Childhood Care and Education - This internet-based Resource Network connects parents to local resources to aid their children’s early development and learning.

  • Integrating Platforms for Human CPS: A Smart Predictive Transit Routing Application - This project improves resident engagement with the public transit system by providing provide context-aware and individualized travel planning simulation for residents using the transit services.
  • SIM: Seismic and Infrastructure Monitoring - This project would provide community resiliency and predictive maintenance for cities.
  • CCI Safe + Smart Cities Information - This initiative helps cities optimize their operational performance and harden their resiliency through the efficient use of technology.
  • Mobile First Service on Demand (ALICE and Gansevoort Hotel Group) - This system would understand, manage, and improve the daily operations of hotel properties through the creation of real time mobile communication technology.
  • Green Infrastructure Monitoring & Assessment Project - This project will quantify stormwater volume reduction to the combined sewer system and demonstrate their impact on combined sewer overflow events and downstream treatment cost reductions.
