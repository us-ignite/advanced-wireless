US Ignite and the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) have teamed-up with the Department of Transportation (DoT), National Science Foundation (NSF), and Health and Human Services (HHS) to create the Global City Teams Challenge, an initiative designed to advance the deployment of Internet of Things (IoT) technologies within a smart city / smart community environment.

Global City Teams Challenge

Why join a Global City Team?
The Challenge is an opportunity for forward-looking communities to partner with public and private organizations to accelerate the deployment of IoT technologies designed to address some of the most pressing challenges facing cities. The Global City Teams Challenge also benefits innovative companies and non-profits by giving them a chance to implement and/or assess their solutions in valuable municipal testbeds, and provides them with exposure to scores of potential new customers. To see a list of current Action Clusters, please click here.


The Global City Teams Challenge includes:

  • Match making between innovators and communities
  • Networking opportunities
  • Collaboration designed to foster interconnected IoT technologies with greater impact
  • Access to testbeds
  • Opportunities to showcase leadership in the development and implementation of IoT

How will the Global City Teams Challenge Work?

NIST and US Ignite will help organize "action clusters," composed of representatives of innovative local governments, non-profits and private companies. These teams volunteer to work collaboratively to build, deploy or test transformative IoT applications. The Global City Teams Challenge builds upon the success of NIST’s 2013-2014 SmartAmerica initiative in which more than 100 organizations participated forming 24 teams.
