2016 Global City Teams Challenge Expo

Participants in the Global City Teams Challenge (GCTC) will present their projects at the 2016 GCTC Expo, to be held at the Austin Convention Center on June 13 and 14, 2016.

The Expo, convened by NIST and US Ignite, will showcase GCTC Action Clusters and will feature speakers and panelists on a wide range of smart city topics. The two-day event will include an exhibition hall in which trade-show spaces will be made available to the Action Clusters. In addition to the opportunity to exhibit, the Action Clusters will be invited to present their project on the main stage.

The 2016 GCTC Expo is free of charge for both exhibitors and attendees.

For more information about the event and to register for the GCTC Expo, please visit www.gctcexpo.org.

Overview Sponsors

The GCTC Expo will be co-located with two other events that will take place at Austin Convention Center June 13-15. Co-locating the 2016 GCTC Expo with the Smart City Innovation Summit and the US Ignite Application Summit will encourage more attendees and participants to travel to Austin and will result in more exposure for GCTC Action Clusters.

Although the GCTC Expo is co-located with US Ignite’s Application Summit and the Smart Cities Innovation Summit, the GCTC Expo is a separate and distinct event. The GCTC Expo will have its own exhibition space and its own process for registration.

2016 GCTC Action Clusters will receive an exhibition space in the GCTC Expo and a speaking slot at no charge, subject to space availability and contingent upon the Action Cluster fulfilling the requirements listed in the GCTC participating guide. Please review (https://www.us-ignite.org/globalcityteams/participation-guide) for more information.

Acceptance as a speaker or exhibitor by the US Ignite Application Summit or the Smart Cities Innovation Summit events does not entitle an organization to speak or exhibit at the GCTC Expo.

If you have questions about the 2016 GCTC Expo, please contact Mariya Cherk at mariya.cherk@us-ignite.org or Sokwoo Rhee at sokwoo.rhee@nist.gov.
